Content

Degree Theory: algebraic (structure of partial order of degrees) and logical (theory of (D,v,≤)), global and local (automorphisms, definability, r.e. and Delta^0_2 degrees), techniques (forcing, determinacy, permitting, priority arguments, full approximation); Hierarchies: jump operator, arithmetical hierarchy, high-/lowness, pseudo-jump operators, second-order arithmetic, effective descriptive set theory, recursive ordinals, hyperarithmetical sets, Kleene's O, hyperjump and beyond, set theoretical hierarchies; algorithmic randomness: information theoretic complexity, information theoretic "halting problem", lowness notions, information theoretic vs computational complexity, randomness and genericity.
