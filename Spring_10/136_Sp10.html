Content

Functions computable by algorithm, Turing machines, Church's thesis. Unsolvability of the halting problem, Rice's theorem. Recursively enumerable sets, creative sets, many-one reductions. Self-referential programs. Godel's incompleteness theorems, undecidability of validity, decidable and undecidable theories.

Text

N. Cutland, Computability: An Introduction to Recursive Function Theory, Cambridge University Press, 1980.

Supplementary reading.

G. Boolos, J. Burgess, R. Jeffrey, Computability and Logic, Cambridge University Press, 2007

S. B. Cooper, Computability Theory, Chapman-Hall, 2003 (more extensive than Cutland, covers advanced topics - good supplementary reading if the subject really interests you)

H. Rogers, Theory of Recursive Functions and Effective Computability, MIT Press, 1987 (excellent graduate text, many exercises)

J. Shoenfield, Mathematical Logic, AK Peters, 2001 (classic logic text on the graduate level, still a benchmark)

M. Sipser, Introduction to the Theory of Computation, Course Technology, 2005 (excellent book, different scope than our course, more emphasis on formal languages and complexity, particularly suited for computer scientists)

A note on academic honesty: Collaboration among students to solve homework assignments is welcome. This is a good way to learn mathematics. So is the consultation of other sources such as other textbooks. However, every student has to hand in an own set of solutions, and if you use other people's work or ideas you should indicate the source in your solutions.
